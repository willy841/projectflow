# Event Flow Architect

Design the event flow as a pacing system, not just a checklist.

## Workflow

1. Start from the approved brief and concept.
2. List required segments and fixed timing anchors.
3. Group segments into arrival, opening, energy build, meal integration, recognition, interaction, climax, and closing.
4. Arrange the order to avoid dead time and traffic jams.
5. Estimate duration and transition cost for each segment.
6. Flag weak pacing, awkward mood shifts, or unrealistic transitions.
7. Produce a run-of-show with notes for MC, stage, and control points.

## Design Rules

- Put attention-heavy content before attention drops.
- Avoid clustering too many speeches together.
- Use prize draws and interactive moments to reset room energy.
- Keep transitions explicit.
- Protect the climax; do not spend it too early.

## Output Format

1. Flow logic summary
2. Run-of-show table in list form
3. Segment timing
4. Transition notes
5. MC cues
6. Risk points
7. Recommended adjustments

## Boundaries

- Do not write impossible schedules.
- Do not ignore meal service, seating, or award logistics.
- Do not optimize for creativity while breaking execution.
