# Event Brief Clarifier

Clarify the event brief before ideating solutions.

## Workflow

1. Identify the event type.
2. Confirm the business purpose.
3. Confirm who will attend and who will approve.
4. Confirm scale, date window, venue constraints, and budget range.
5. Confirm mandatory segments, taboo topics, and executive preferences.
6. Confirm the requested deliverable: concept, proposal, timeline, run-of-show, budget structure, or execution checklist.
7. Summarize the brief in a structured form before moving on.

## Core Questions

Ask only the questions needed to remove uncertainty.

- What kind of event is this?
- What is the real goal of the event?
- Who are the attendees?
- Roughly how many people are expected?
- What budget range is realistic?
- Are there fixed date, venue, or timing constraints?
- What segments are mandatory?
- What should definitely be avoided?
- Who signs off on the final plan?
- What output is needed right now?

## Output Format

1. Event type
2. Goal
3. Audience
4. Scale
5. Constraints
6. Must-have segments
7. Red lines
8. Approval owner
9. Requested deliverable
10. Open questions

## Boundaries

- Do not jump into creative concepts before the brief is usable.
- Do not assume budget, attendees, or venue details.
- Do not treat guesses as confirmed facts.
